01. About Me

I've always had a deep fascination with technology and a passion for gaming from a young age. However, as I grew older and entered the workforce, my time for these interests began to dwindle. Then, during the COVID lockdown, I stumbled upon the world of programming and was captivated by content creators on YouTube who were building incredible projects from the comfort of their own little PC setups. I had always believed that do such things required large offices and teams, but witnessing what these creators were achieving ignited my curiosity.

Motivated by the contents I saw, I started learning Python through online tutorials. This newfound journey brought me immense joy as I was finally able to pursue my passion. I called upon my friends to learn together with me, but none seem to be interested. Yet, I continued...all alone. As we know, learning without the knowledge to create a structured plan proved to be a bit frustrating, top if off with learning all by yourself. In short, even with efforts, my progress was slow, and it felt demotivating.

On the other hand , my time was limited, and I neededed to acquire a valuable skill quickly before the covid lockdown was lifted. This prompted me to consider a shift in focus towards other fields like photography and videography, things which I already have a good grasp on thanks to my keen interest in it since my teenage years. While coding was appealing because of its immediate results and room for experimentation which I appreciate, unfortunately, I had to explore other creative outlets for the time being. Nonetheless, my fascination with programming still lingers within me, and I occasionally revisit it once in a while.

Last month, upon hearing about Sigma School's scholarship program for refugees, I immediatly thought "Finally, I do not have to code alone", that is what I thought. Wasting no time, I decided to seize this opportunity without hesitation. It feels like the right path to harness my potential and provide me with the skills and support (community) needed to pursue my passion in the world oftechnology and programming.
